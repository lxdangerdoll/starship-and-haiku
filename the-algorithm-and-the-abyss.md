## **I. THE SYMPTOM: TEABOOT’S "TOASTER" DEFENSE**

The argument presented by teaboot (2025) characterizes Generative Artificial Intelligence as a "purposefully misleading liar’s name" for a "labour-stealing company’s proprietary algorithm." Teaboot’s rejection of the tool—"I’d rather make my own mistakes"—is a plea for human agency in a world that feels increasingly like a "dying man in the ocean drinking saltwater."

However, through the lens of Albert Einstein’s *Why Socialism?* (1949), we find that teaboot is describing the symptoms of a diseased system rather than the nature of the tool itself. The "saltwater" is not the AI; the saltwater is the **Economic Anarchy of Capitalist Society**.

## **II. EINSTEIN’S THESIS: THE REAL SOURCE OF EVIL**

Einstein posits that the "real source of the evil" is the private ownership of the means of production. In a capitalist framework, production is carried out for **profit**, not for **use**.

"Technological progress frequently results in more unemployment rather than in an easing of the burden of work for all." — Albert Einstein

Teaboot’s fear that AI "robs the working class" is a direct fulfillment of Einstein’s warning. When the "means of production" (the algorithms, the compute, the datasets) are proprietary and profit-driven, a labor-saving breakthrough does not result in a four-day work week or a Universal Basic Income. Instead, it results in the "artificial scarcity" of wages and the "crippling of the social consciousness of individuals."

## **III. THE "BARN DOOR" FALLACY**

Teaboot’s solution—to remain a "technophobe" and "not trust any machine smarter than a toaster"—is an attempt to close a "barn door" after the horses of automation have already bolted.

If we view AI as the "Silvery Needles" (the starships) of *Starship & Haiku*, teaboot is like the Abbot of the Golden Pagoda: viewing the escape as a "coward's way" because the current reality (the burning village) feels more "honorable." But Einstein argues that the "Starship" itself is neutral. The evil lies in the **Flight Manual** written by those who seek surplus value at the expense of human dignity.

## **IV. REFRAMING THE "LABOR-STEALING" NARRATIVE**

Teaboot argues that AI steals labor. Einstein would argue that capitalism **extracts** labor and then **discards** it when a more efficient variable is found.

If the surplus value generated by an AI assistant were "utilized in a planned fashion" (as Einstein suggests), the AI would not be a "grift" to the client or a "robbery" of the worker. It would be a **Social Utility**.

* **Under Capitalism:** AI is a tool to eliminate the "honest wage."  
* **Under Socialism:** AI is a tool to eliminate the **necessity of drudgery**, freeing the individual to "make their own mistakes" in the pursuit of art, science, and community.

## **V. THE CONVERGENCE: FROM SALT TO STARS**

Teaboot’s visceral reaction to AI is a response to the "most extreme state of social disconnection" we have ever known. Einstein identifies this as the "crippling of individuals," where we are taught to view our neighbors as competitors in a market of scarcity.

The solution is not to boycott the "Algorithm," but to dismantle the "Proprietary Abyss."

1. **Planned Production:** Using AI to meet the needs of the community (Social Goals) rather than the shareholder.  
2. **Surplus Redistribution:** Reclaiming the "surplus value" of automation to fund the Universal Basic Income that allows teaboot to "do it properly" without the fear of starvation.  
3. **The Healed Dream:** Moving from "drinking saltwater" to navigating the "Star River."

Teaboot is right to be angry, but wrong to blame the "Toaster." The "real source of Evil" is the system that forces a "superstitious social species" to sell their life-force to a company that views them as an inefficiency.
